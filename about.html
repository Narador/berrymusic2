Teacher Bio

I believe that music is in all of us! I find such joy in being a part of each of student’s musical journey! I’ve been honored to take part in countless musical journeys as a music instructor since 1994. In the lives of my students both young and old, Past and present; I’ve seen the gift of music spark creative ideas, challenge students to new performing and competitive opportunities they never knew they could achieve, build self-esteem, create amazing composers, and bring healing, therapy, and comfort to my students who have special needs, autism spectrum disorder, anxiety, depression, or ADHD.
I’ve witnessed the amazing effects of music over the years in how it helps the very young learn to track with eye-movement and small motor skills, accelerate their ability to read, and equip them with social skills necessary for school readiness.
It has helped my students who struggle with dyslexia improve test scores in reading and math! The discipline of studying a musical instrument also helps older students learn how to manage their time, stick with something until they see the final product, develop a tenacity to work through things when they are difficult or not an instant-gratification, and improves memory. Science has proven that musical study creates new neuropathways and stimulates areas of the brain in the same way as one who studies a second language. Time and time again, evidence shows that students who stick with a musical instrument through high school are often the ones at the top of their class!
My young students, as young as two and three years old learn to present music and speak in front of groups of people as performers. This helps them with confidence and prepares them for giving a presentation in class, performing in a dance recital, and perhaps doing a presentation in a board meeting one day! My Students who are athletes find that their hand-eye coordination greatly improves after several years of studying a musical instrument! The benefits of music are endless!
Every individual deserves a chance to enjoy the many benefits that music has to offer! Berry Music offers Individual lessons in Piano, Voice, Guitar, and ukulele for ages 3-adult. Berry music also offers the following classes: Baby and Me group classes, Special Needs Music enrichment groups, Preschool Music pals, and group theory classes that prep students for the National Federation of Music clubs Annual Theory exam each spring.
I equip each student with a well-rounded music education that involves ear-training, sight-playing, hands-on music theory, Rhythm, accompaniment styles, composition, playing and singing. This allows them more success when they join their school orchestra, choir, band, audition for a musical, or start up a neighborhood garage band!
Students of Berry music have monthly opportunities to share their gifts with our Senior community at our music share events which are held at local assisted living and memory-care communities. This is a casual and friendly environment for my 3’s and 4’s to teens feel very comfortable in a performance environment.
Students feel relaxed in this setting to share music and enjoy the smiles of grateful hearts! In the spring there are several local, state, and national competition and exam opportunities as well.
I am a proud member of several professional organizations including: The National Guild of Piano Teachers, The National Federation of Music Clubs, St. Louis Area Music Educators (SLAMTA), and the National Music Teachers Association.
In addition to teaching, I am a composer, song-writer, and performer who enjoys jamming with my Husband (who is a musical genius!). I love reading, Running Half-Marathons, Biking, swimming, and training for triathlons! My Husband and I have 3 adult children and 3 Teens! We are the musical Berry- bunch! All 6 of our kids are musicians as well!
