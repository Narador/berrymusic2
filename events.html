In my own life, Music has been a refuge and a healing for me. Music can be very soothing for those who suffer from chronic pain, anxiety, depression, ADHD, Autism Spectrum disorder, Dyslexia, and other special needs, and memory loss.
There is a strong connection between rhythm skills and pre-reading ability. Toddlers are naturally Musical if you let them be. Movement and music go hand-in hand. Music is first Heard, then felt, then read.
Children who study a musical instrument or a language stimulate a different region of the brain than those who do not study a second language or music.
Children who study music have higher test scores in reading and math, develop healthy study habits, learn fine-motor development and eye-tracking skills which increase hand-eye coordination. Musicians excel at their sport due to the increase in hand-eye coordination and motor development. Children who study music and perform develop a healthy self-esteem, good social skills, the ability the problem-solve, the ability to multitask, the ability set short and long term goals,
